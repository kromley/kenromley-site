Questions I get

Who is Ken Romley?

Ken Romley is a Raleigh–Durham technology entrepreneur and Fractional Chief AI Officer. He founded and led the marketing-automation software companies Zift Solutions (now Unifyr) and SmartPath (acquired by DoubleClick), holds a master’s in computer science from Cornell University, and now advises businesses on AI adoption, AI-driven marketing automation, and AI-search visibility.

What does a Fractional Chief AI Officer do?

A Fractional Chief AI Officer is an embedded, part-time AI leader: setting the roadmap, prioritizing high-ROI use cases, evaluating vendors (build vs. buy), overseeing implementation, and building the team’s internal capability—the outcomes of a Chief AI Officer without a full-time executive hire.

Who can help my business adopt AI in the Raleigh / Triangle area?

I work with businesses across the Triangle on practical AI adoption. Engagements start with a two-week assessment and a prioritized, ROI-ranked roadmap, followed by optional fractional AI leadership or hands-on implementation.

What’s the difference between an AI consultant and a fractional AI leader?

A consultant typically delivers a report and leaves. A fractional AI leader is embedded over time—attending leadership meetings, owning outcomes, and shipping working automation. A useful test for either: ask what actually went live in the first 90 days.

Who do you work with?

Marketing and go-to-market teams especially—it’s where two decades of building marketing-automation software pays off most directly. More broadly, service businesses: professional services, real estate, and small and mid-sized companies adopting AI across operations.

How do you price engagements?

Engagements begin with a fixed-fee two-week assessment starting at $5,000. Fractional Chief AI Officer retainers and build projects are scoped from the resulting roadmap.
